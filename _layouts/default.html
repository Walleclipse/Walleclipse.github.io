Abulikemu Abuduweili

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering and Robotics Institute, at Carnegie Mellon University. I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Changliu Liu, in the Robotics Institute, School of Computer Science. Before joining CMU, I spent an enriching year as a research assistant with Prof. Humphrey Shi at the University of Oregon. I hold a Bachelor's and a Master's degree from the School of Electronics Engineering and Computer Science at Peking University. My research interests lie in robot learning, human-robot collaboration, optimization, continual learning, data-efficient machine learning, and computer vision.

I have also gained valuable industry experience through internships at several organizations. In the summer of 2024, I worked on Visual Representation Learning for Robotics at Microsoft Research Redmond, collaborating with Lekan Molu and Naoki Wake. During the summer of 2023, I focused on Diffusion Models at the Toyota Research Institute, where I worked closely with Frank Permenter and Chenyang Yuan. Before 2021, I conducted a research internship at Baidu Research under the guidance of Prof. Dejing Dou. I also completed a research internship at Microsoft Research Asia, collaborating with Qingwei Lin.

Feel free to reach out for collaborations or to discuss research. My official name is Abulikemu Abuduweili, but I also go by Abduwali /ˌæb du wæ li/ or Abu /ˈɑ bu/.

I am actively seeking a full-time, research-oriented position. If my expertise aligns with your needs, please feel free to reach out.
