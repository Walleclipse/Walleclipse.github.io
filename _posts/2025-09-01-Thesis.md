How do we build robots that keep learning throughout their lifetimeâ€”without forgetting what they already know? My doctoral thesis tackles this grand challenge head-on. Real-world robots face streaming data in ever-changing environments, yet conventional machine learning crumbles under three critical barriers: catastrophic forgetting (robots unlearn old skills when acquiring new ones), severe data hunger (learning from scratch is impractical), and the difficulty of modeling complex dynamics on the fly. This work presents principled algorithms that overcome each barrier, bringing us closer to truly autonomous, lifelong-learning robots.
