Koopman operator theory lifts nonlinear dynamics into a higher-dimensional space where the evolution is linear, enabling classical linear control (LQR, MPC) on complex nonlinear systems. But how do prediction and control errors scale with the latent dimension and the number of training samples? We study this across 8 nonlinear systems—pendulums, robot arms, humanoids, and quadrupeds—and establish a scaling law governing the data-model tradeoff. We prove that the optimal data-model scaling follows m = c n ln(n), predicting the minimum data needed for a given model size to achieve near-optimal error, validated empirically across all environments.
