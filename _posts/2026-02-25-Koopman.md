We study how the prediction and control errors of neural Koopman models scale with latent dimension and training data across 8 nonlinear systems. We establish a scaling law showing the optimal data-model tradeoff follows m = c n ln(n), validated on pendulums, robot arms, humanoids, and quadrupeds.
